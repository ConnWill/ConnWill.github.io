My One True Love

The second I saw it, I knew it was the one. 100 yards, two end zones, two goalposts, 22 players, and nonstop contact for

60 minutes. What's not there to love about football? Chicago is blessed with a sports team in every major American sport,

but no one captures the love and the energy of the city quite like the Chicago Bears.



My father has had season tickets

since he was a kid, and they were passed down to him by his late father. Growing up when I wasn't playing baseball I was

eating, sleeping, and breathing Bears football. Needless to say I could not wait to strap on the pads. Whether it was

Pee-Wee, Pop Warner, or High School ball there wasn't a second where I didn't enjoy it. I was never the fastest or

strongest person on the field during high school ball and I didn't play all that much, but that didn't matter because the very

first day of freshman football changed my life. I went to the biggest school in each district I was in, so when I arrived at

my high school of 4000+ kids on the first day I wasn't worried about making friends. I had a group of friends from middle

school, and we hung out with the kids from the other schools so by the time I got to high school I was practically set. My

tight knit circle of friends from high school is a rather large group of about 15 guys. I was only friends with 2 of them

before I met the other 10+ guys either in football or through mutual friends from football. When it came to skill, as I said

I wasn't the very best so therefore I did not start. In fact I was the scout team quarterback, so every week I ran our

opponents offense against the first string defense. It was during high school football when I truly realized the importance

of the scout team. Certain guys complained after being put on scout team for the week, but I embraced it. It was our job

as the scout team to fully prepare the starters for the coming week. However just because I embraced the scout team does

not mean I was satisfied, So I decided to try a new sport, one that went from Early Uncertainty to Present Perfection.
