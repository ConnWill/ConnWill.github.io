Learning to Love but Also to Hate

In some ways I could say baseball was my first love, but that was really only the case because I could start playing the

earliest, which was coincidentally the year after my team, The Chicago White Sox won a World Series.



I started playing Little League when I was 5 years old, which for my city was the Tee-ball league. After Tee-Ball was

"coach pitch", then farm league, C league, B league and finally, the A league. My passion for the game and drive to be

better really jumped into gear when I had an opportunity to skip from C league to A league. The managers for the teams

held a draft every year to decide teams. It was at that draft where I skipped the B League and began to take myself

seriously, and for that very reason it was at that draft where my love for baseball began to crumble.

TRAVEL TEAMS, HITTING LESSONS, PITCHING LESSONS where does it end? Between the ages of 7-10 I spent

countless hours at lessons, countless weekends at tournaments, and very little time to remember exactly why I was doing

it. After the age of 10-11 my interest in playing and in many cases watching baseball came to an end, and I never played

it again. It wasn't all bad though, excelling at the game of baseball taught me that hard work really does pay off, and

sooner or later you will enjoy the countless hours perfecting your craft.

I was going to find that One True Love.
